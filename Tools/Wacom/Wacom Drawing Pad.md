Wacom Drawing Pad: How to drag a window, e,g. PowerShell around?

Using a **Wacom drawing tablet** to drag windows (like PowerShell) works a bit differently than with a mouse—but once set up, it feels very natural 👍

***

## 🖱️ Basic Method (Works Immediately)

To drag a window:

1. **Move the pen** over the tablet → moves the cursor

2. **Hover over the window title bar** (top of PowerShell window)

3. **Touch the tablet with the pen tip** (this is your “left click”)

4. **Keep the pen pressed down**

5. **Move the pen** → the window will follow

6. **Lift the pen** → drop the window

👉 Think of it like: _pen touching = mouse button held down_

***

## ⚙️ If It Doesn’t Work Well (Common Fixes)

### 1. Check Pen Mode

Open **Wacom Tablet Properties**:

* Make sure you're in **Pen Mode** (absolute positioning)

* Not “Mouse Mode” (can feel weird for dragging)

***

### 2. Adjust Click Sensitivity

If dragging fails or releases too early:

* Go to **Pen settings**

* Lower the **Tip Feel** (make it softer)

This makes it easier to "hold" the click while moving.

***

### 3. Use the Side Button (Recommended ⭐)

Most Wacom pens have a button:

* Assign one button to **“Click & Drag”** or **“Left Click”**

* Then:

  * Hover pen

  * Press side button

  * Move pen → smooth dragging

👉 This is MUCH easier than pressing the tip while moving.

***

### 4. Windows Ink Setting

Sometimes helpful:

* Enable or disable **Windows Ink** in Wacom settings

* Try both—depends on your system

***

## 🧠 Pro Tip (Feels Like Magic)

If you're using multiple monitors:

* Map the tablet to **only one screen**\
  → improves precision dramatically

***

## 🧪 Quick Test

Try dragging:

* A **PowerShell window**

* A **browser tab**

* A **file in Explorer**

If one works and another doesn’t → it's likely a Windows setting, not Wacom.
